Fractal Truss Analysis Using the Joint Method

This interactive tool demonstrates the concept of analyzing forces in a truss structure using fractals and the joint method.

The joint method, also known as the method of joints, is a process used to determine the forces in individual members of a truss structure. The primary principle behind the joint method is that the sum of forces in any direction for a joint in equilibrium is zero. This means the algebraic sum of all horizontal forces and all vertical forces acting on the joint is zero. By applying this method systematically to all joints, we can determine the forces acting on each member of the truss.
Fractals are complex structures that look similar at any level of magnification. They are formed by repeating a simple process in an ongoing feedback loop. In this code, we use fractals to generate complex truss structures by recursively dividing triangles into smaller triangles. This allows for the creation of intricate and varied truss designs using a simple algorithmic approach.
